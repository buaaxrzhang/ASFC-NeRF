ASFC-NeRF: Large-Scale Scene Rendering with Adaptive Sampling and Feature-aware Compression

Xinrui Zhang1, Yufeng Wang1, Shuangkang Fang1, Zesheng Wang1, Huayu Zhang1, Dacheng Qi1, Wenrui Ding1,
1Beihang University
Paper Data(coming)

Abstract

While significant progress has been made in large-scale scene representation using Neural Radiance Fields (NeRF), several limitations remain. For instance, most methods still rely on the original coarse-to-fine sampling strategy, leading to an inefficient rendering process. Additionally, to model larger scenes, these methods often use complex network models, resulting in redundant model parameters.

To address these issues, we propose a novel model with adaptive sampling and feature-aware compression for large-scale scene rendering, named ASFC-NeRF. We first introduce a weight prediction network to replace the original coarse sampling strategy, then employ a teacher network and depth constraints for knowledge distillation in the early stages of training to enhance the high-fidelity of the scene. Furthermore, we optimize the number of Grids and the channels of Planes and prune the network to efficiently compress model parameters.

Experimental results demonstrate that our method significantly accelerates the rendering process and greatly reduces parameter quantity while maintaining or only slightly lowering image quality. Therefore, ASFC-NeRF exhibits advantages in comprehensive performance and practicality.

Method

The pipeline of ASFC-NeRF. Initially, we pre-train a scene representation teacher network with the original coarse-to-fine sampling way, which involves two model inferences. Subsequently, a weight prediction network is introduced to replace the coarse inference and apply knowledge distillation based on the depth information of the teacher network. In the later stages of training, the student network no longer relies on supervision from the teacher network but engages in self-supervision based on the fine inference results. Red points represent coarse sampling points while blue points represent fine sampling points. Additionally, we optimize the feature parameters for different scenes and prune the MLP to compress model parameters.

Results

Quantitative comparisons

Quantitative results on PSNR, SSIM, LPIPS and average training time. Compared with vanilla NeRF, Instant-NGP and Mega-NeRF, our method achieves highest image quality in the shortest time.

Qualitative comparisons

Qualitative comparisons on several datasets. Our method maintains the visual quality while accelerating rendering and compressing storage.
